## A wallet is just a key to your money

In Module 1, we established that your bank balance is just a number in someone else's database. A crypto wallet flips that — it's a number in a *public* database, and **you hold the only key.**

No bank can freeze it. No app maintenance window can lock you out. No third party sits between you and your money.

That's what "self-custody" means. You custody your own funds. Like cash in your hand, but digital.

## The old way sucked

Traditional crypto wallets came with a **seed phrase** — 12 or 24 random words you had to write down and never lose. Lose the words, lose your money. Forever. No recovery. No helpdesk.

This was the barrier. Not the technology, not the concept — the UX. Asking normal people to manage 24 words on a piece of paper like it's 1997 was never going to scale.

## Coinbase Smart Wallet changed this

Coinbase Smart Wallet is a **self-custody wallet with no seed phrase.** It uses the same passkey technology that unlocks your phone — FaceID, fingerprint, or screen lock.

Here's what that means in practice:

| | Old-school wallet | Coinbase Smart Wallet |
|---|---|---|
| **Setup time** | 10–15 min | Under 2 min |
| **Seed phrase** | 24 words, write them down, pray | None |
| **Authentication** | Passwords, extensions, hardware | FaceID / fingerprint / passkey |
| **Recovery** | Lose the phrase = lose everything | Passkey recovery via your device |
| **Runs on** | Browser extensions, dedicated apps | Any browser, mobile or desktop |
| **Cost to create** | Gas fees on some networks | Free |

You get the security of self-custody without the anxiety of a seed phrase.

## Self-custody vs exchange custody — the one-paragraph version

When your crypto sits on an exchange (Coinbase the exchange, Binance, etc.), they hold the keys. It's convenient, but it's their database — same model as a bank. If they get hacked, go bankrupt, or freeze withdrawals, your funds are stuck. Self-custody means **you** hold the keys. The tradeoff used to be complexity. Smart Wallets eliminated that tradeoff.

## How to set up your wallet

1. **Go to [carbon-contractors.com](https://carbon-contractors.com)** and hit Register.
2. **Tap "Create Wallet"** — this triggers the Coinbase Smart Wallet flow.
3. **Authenticate with your passkey** — FaceID, fingerprint, or screen lock. Whatever your device supports.
4. **Done.** You now have a wallet on Base (Coinbase's L2 network). Zero gas fees for transactions.

No app to download. No extension to install. No seed phrase to lose. Your passkey *is* your key.

## What just happened under the hood

When you created that wallet, three things were set up:

- **A wallet address** — your public identifier on the blockchain. Think of it like a BSB and account number, but universal.
- **A passkey** — stored securely on your device, used to sign transactions. This replaces the seed phrase.
- **A smart contract account** — your wallet is actually a contract on Base, which means it can do things traditional wallets can't (like gasless transactions and recovery options).

You don't need to understand any of this to use it. But if you're the type who wants to know what's under the bonnet — now you know.
